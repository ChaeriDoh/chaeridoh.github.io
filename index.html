About

I started my career as a Teach For America educator and found my way to economics — not because it was a straight line, but because I kept chasing the question of how to make complex ideas land for real people.

At the New York Fed, I’ve built that into a full practice: creating educational content, managing flagship programs, presenting at schools, and using technology and data to reach more people more effectively.

Personal Projects
🔥 The Phoenix 🖤 The Phantom

We got married on February 22, 2020 — and had to cancel our Hawaii honeymoon when the world locked down a month later. So instead, we turned our Rochelle Park living room into a bike workshop.

My husband’s engineering instincts sparked the idea: build our bikes from scratch. We studied bicycle anatomy, built color-coded spreadsheets tracking every part, source, and cost, then ordered custom carbon fiber frames with hand-painted designs.

My frame: a sunrise fade in metallic red and orange — a phoenix rising from the ashes of a pandemic. His: matte black with cherry red, and a hidden hexagon painted inside — the molecular structure of carbon itself.

We finished by riding 20 miles in Burlington, VT on one of the most beautiful trails we’ve ever seen.
